## Questions to ask (grouped, in priority order)

### A. Team & roles — *the thing you flagged*
- Who's on the team today, and who's joining? Walk me through everyone.
- **There's another designer — what's their scope and availability?** Part-time? What have they already put together, and is it a starting point I build on or a parallel track?
- How do we split design work so we're not stepping on each other — who owns what surface?
- Who's the final design decision-maker when the other designer and I disagree — you, or design-led?
- Which partner is non-technical? (Plan assumes one is — it shapes how I present work.)
- Until you hire a PM, who's wearing the PM hat — me, you, or shared? *(Plan currently assumes shared with you.)*

### B. Scope — the 3 MVP pain points (the must-get)
- If we solve exactly **three** homeowner pain points for the MVP, which three? Push for specifics.
- For each: what does "solved" look like to you?
- What's explicitly **out** of v1 so I don't over-build? (e.g., contractor flow depth, municipalities, mobile project management.)
- Mobile: still notifications-only companion in v1, no project management on phone — confirm?

### C. Cadence & comms
- Hours: are we aligned on roughly full-time (~40 hrs/wk) for the sprint?
- Sprint reviews: weekly to start, moving to biweekly later — does that match your expectation?
- Slack as the single channel, with feedback **batched** (one feedback day) rather than live micro-comments — does that work for you and the partners?
- Who from your side needs to be at each review, and who can I move forward without?

### D. Validation & data
- Homeowner interviews in Week 1 — you said recruiting is "not difficult." Who actually recruits them, and can you line up 5–7 by mid-week?
- The aggregated-data moat (timeline/cost/rejection data per AHJ) — **medium-confidence bet.** How early do you want to start instrumenting for it vs. treating it as later?
- The "AI as a visible companion" call for homeowners (citations, attribution, plain-language reasoning) is also a **medium-confidence bet** — are you bought in, or should we A/B it in the first user test?

### E. Success criteria — *come with a proposal, not a blank*
- Propose: time-to-clarity, free→paid conversion, pre-submission validation accuracy, return rate / referrals — beyond raw adoption. Which matter most to you and to the VCs?

### F. Logistics / runway
- Brand: assuming no existing brand and visual direction emerges via the Claude + shadcn workflow — any brand constraints, colors, or names I should know?
- Is mock data + live Claude prompts acceptable for the VC prototype, or do you want any real data wired in?
- Who is the VC audience and what's their timeline? It shapes how I sequence the hi-fi.
